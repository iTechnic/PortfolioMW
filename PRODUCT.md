# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

UK small-business owners: tradespeople (plumbers, electricians, builders), salons and barbers, independent takeaways and food, and similar local services. They are often not tech-confident, are cautious about spending real money, and usually arrive on a phone. The job they are trying to get done: decide whether to trust one person with how their business looks online, without a big upfront leap of faith.

## Product Purpose

A personal-brand service site for Maks Wittenbeck, who builds websites for small businesses. It sells a demo-first process: the client sends an idea, gets a free working demo within days, refines it together, and pays only after they approve the direction, at which point the full site is built and launched. Success is a qualified enquiry via the form, email, or WhatsApp. Credibility comes from five live, openable projects (the PacePot web app plus four trade demo sites), so the site argues by proof rather than promises.

## Brand Personality

Bold, distinctive, warm. Confident and memorable, carried by a strong visual idea and a human voice, not safe minimalism. It should feel like one real person who is genuinely good at design and easy to talk to, never like a faceless agency. Warmth keeps the boldness approachable for a cautious, non-technical buyer.

## Anti-references

- The generic AI/SaaS landing template: indigo-gradient hero, endless rounded feature cards, uppercase eyebrow labels above every section, "trusted by" logo strips. Interchangeable and machine-made.
- Cheap template small-business sites (Wix/GoDaddy stock-photo-and-clipart), which are the exact amateur look the service sells against.
- Cold corporate "digital solutions agency" with buzzwords and no visible human.

## Design Principles

- **Show, don't tell.** Every claim is backed by a live site the visitor can open. Proof beats adjectives.
- **Practice what you preach.** This site is the strongest example of what a client gets; if it looks generic, the pitch collapses.
- **One human, not an agency.** Keep it personal and first-person. The person you email is the person who builds it.
- **Memorable beats safe.** Commit to a distinctive visual idea. Avoid the AI-default SaaS look even when it is the comfortable choice.
- **Low-risk framing.** The demo-first, pay-after-approval promise removes buyer anxiety; the design should reassure, never pressure.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA across the site: body text contrast >= 4.5:1 (large text >= 3:1), visible keyboard focus, full keyboard operability, `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives, and semantic markup. The audience skews older and less tech-confident, so favour high legibility, generous tap targets, and plain language.
