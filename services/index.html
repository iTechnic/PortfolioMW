Services

What I build, and how it works.

Everything here follows the same rule: you see a working demo before you commit to anything, and you get a fixed quote, not an hourly rate.

01

Business websites

The full picture of your business online: services, location, opening hours, proof, and contact details, organised the way customers actually look for them. Built page by page around what your customers need to know before they pick up the phone, with search basics handled so local people can find you.

02

Landing pages

One offer, one audience, one next step. A focused page for a specific service, a seasonal promotion, or an ad campaign that needs somewhere better to land than your homepage. Fast to build and easy to measure.

03

Mobile-first design

Most local customers arrive on a phone, often in a hurry. Every layout I make starts on a small screen: readable text, thumb-sized buttons, phone numbers that dial on tap, and pages that load before the customer loses patience. Desktop gets the expanded version, not the other way round.

04

Redesign concepts

Already have a website that underperforms? I build a sharper version alongside your current one so you can compare them side by side, with real content, on a real link. If the new version does not clearly win, you have lost nothing.

02

Straight answers on money.

How does pricing work?

Fixed quote, agreed before any work starts. The demo comes first, so by the time you are deciding, you are looking at the actual site rather than an estimate and a promise.

What does the quote include?

Design, build, content shaping, mobile layout, search basics, and getting the site live. No surprise line items at the end.

What about after launch?

Two options. I can keep looking after the site, updates, small changes, and keeping it fast, for a small monthly amount. Or I hand everything over fully and you own it outright. Your choice, no lock-in.

What if I do not like the demo?

Then you say so and walk away. The demo exists so you can judge the real thing before committing. You only pay for a site you have already seen working.
